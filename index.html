Day 23 of Tracking

Daily Update, April 13, 2020

4902 tests reported in PA today. 1366 positive. 17 deaths were reported today. Montgomery County has the second highest number of confirmed positive cases in the state by county, but it ranks 8th in % of county infected. Monroe county ranks first with 0.48% of their population testing positive. It is early to say, but this may be the beginning of seeing how county wealth affects spread. Montgomery County ranks 2nd in the state in per capita income, while Monroe county ranks 21st.

Repeating from yesterday because I think it is important: A month into wide spread testing in the state, it seems as though the number of tests adminisitered is less important. Medical professionals seem to have a good understanding of the symptoms associated with Covid-19. For many patients there is no need to test, and a greater need to understand severity and treatment. The number of positive cases reported will continue to serve as an estimate to how the virus is spreading, but only if the number of tests per day stays somewhat consistent.

Day 16 of Tracking

Daily Update, April 6, 2020

6083 tests reported in PA today. 1470 positive. 12 deaths were reported today. Philadelphia County reported 32% of the positive cases in the last 24 hours. Both Luzerne and Lehigh county reported 9% of the postive cases today. Throughout my 16 days of tracking, Montogomery and Philadelphia county have been the leaders in total positive cases. This is still the case, but the number of cases in Lehigh County is increasing rapidly. There are now over 1000 cases in Lehigh County, the third highest in the state. I am wondering why this could be. Lehigh County only has a population of 300k. Could be many reasons, but it is interesting to note that Lehigh county is home to the 3rd largest hospital, Lehigh Valley Hospital, by number of hospital beds. (SOURCE) It is possible and important to note that the state is reporting cases not by permanent residence, but rather by site of positive test.
