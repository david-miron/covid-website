Tracking COVID-19 in Pennsylvania

Created by David Miron

Intro

Page last updated on, April 1, 2020

As I am quarantined in PA, I have been tracking the development of COVID-19 in the state. All data is pulled from health.pa.gov on a daily basis. All opinions are my own. This website is mainly to serve as a place for organzing findings and data.

Day 11 of Tracking

Daily Update, April 1, 2020

5744 tests reported in PA today. 962 positive. 11 deaths were reported today. Since the first death was reported in PA (3.19.2020) there has been an averag of 5 deaths reported per day. Like other metropolitan areas, Philadelphia has become a hot spot for the virus. As NYC is to New York state, it appears Philadelphia is to Pennsylvania (in regard to the virus and on a much smaller scale). Allegheny County, home to the second largest city in PA (Pittsburgh), is about 3/4 the size of Pennsylvania County in Population and has 1/4 the number of positive cases as Philadelphia County (Pittsburgh has 1/5 the population of Philadelphia).

Day 10 of Tracking

Daily Update, March 31, 2020

4843 tests reported in PA today. 756 positive. 60 of 68 counties are now testing in PA. 14 deaths were reported today. 77% of hospitalizations in PA are among adults 50+ (since 3.6.2020 there are 514 hospitalizations. Growth rate of positive cases in Philadelphia county continues to increase. Montogomery county showing less signs on increased growth. It is concerning that there less than 5000 tests were reported today. It is important that the state can increase its testing capactiy, but has failed to do so at scale so far.

Day 9 of Tracking

Daily Update, March 30, 2020

4087 tests reported in PA today. 693 positive. 59 of 68 counties are now testing in PA. 11 deaths were reported today. 74% of hospitalizations in PA are among adults 50+ (as of 3.6.2020 there are 386 hospitalizations. The growth rate has increased for 4 straight days, while tests reported has stayed consistent. This will be a telling week, and I hope that we see incresased testing.

Day 8 of Tracking

Daily Update, March 29, 2020

5450 tests reported in PA today. 643 positive. 58 of 68 counties are now testing in PA. 4 deaths were reported today. 46% of positive cases in PA are among adults 50+. 76% of hospitalizations in PA are among adults 50+ (as of 3.6.2020 there are 353 hospitalizations. Slight up-tick in growth rate at 12% today (highest it has been since wider testing began).

Day 7 of Tracking

Daily Update, March 28, 2020

4771 tests reported in PA today. 533 positive. 56 of 68 counties are now testing in PA. 12 deaths were reported today (the most in one day so far). 39% of positive cases in PA are among adults 25-49. 49% of hospitalizations in PA are among adults 65+ (as of 3.6.2020 there are 316 hospitalizations. .05% of Montgomery County has tested positive for Coronavirus.

Day 6 of Tracking

Daily Update, March 27, 2020

5106 tests reported in PA today. 531 positive. 50 of 68 counties are now testing in PA. 6 deaths were reported today. Again, looking at NY State, this is the most important time to continue best practices for prevention. When New York had 22k administered tests, only 4k were positive. Now the state has 37k confirmed cases. There is plenty of room for growth within PA, and with it good reason to continue quarantine. Philadelphia and Montgomery County continue to report the most positive cases with 128 and 92, respectively. Interesting new stats: 40% of positive cases in PA are among adults 25-49. 48% of hospitalizations in PA are among adults 65+. PA has started to release stats on age breakdowns. Please find them at health.pa.gov.

Day 5 of Tracking

Daily Update, March 26, 2020

5808 tests reported in PA today. 560 positive. 48 of 68 counties are now testing in PA. 5 deaths were reported today. Wow! This is a huge developlent day with more than 5k tests reported. Numbers are overwhelmingly positive for the state but we are just at the beginning of preventing very wide spread. Looking at NYC, this is the most important time to continue best practices for prevention. Philadelphia and Montgomery County continue to report the most positive cases with 145 and 110, respectively. Unfortunately, there is not information being provided on how many tests each county is conducting. This would let us know the number of negatives by county, and better inform the us of the concentration of the virus.

Day 4 of Tracking

Daily Update, March 25, 2020

2826 tests in PA today. 276 positive. 44 of 68 counties are now testing in PA. Those counties account for 92% of the population. Philadelphia recorded it's first death due to the virus today. Philadelphia has the most confirmed cases at 257. Only three counties reported more than 20 new cases today: Allegheny County (30), Montgomery County (28), and Philadelphia County (80). It is still early, but all of the numbers for PA are positive when compared to New York.

Day 3 of Tracking

Daily Update, March 24, 2020

2255 tests today. 207 positive. Tests reported per day is increasing at an average of 40% over the last 5 days. 1 more confirmed death today. Philadelphia has the most positive cases with 177. Montgomery County is a close second at 144 cases confirmed. 40 of 68 counties are now reporting.

Day 2 of Tracking

Daily Update, March 23, 2020

1796 tests today. 165 positive. 9% of all tests positive up from 8% yesterday. Monroe and Montgomery counties fasting growing positive cases with 42 positive tests today in both. 6 total deaths in PA now - 2 in Northampton county.

Day 1 of Tracking
